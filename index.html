P2: Hamlet Tableau

2020-04-04

We did a tableau as a project on the Shakespearean play Hamlet. The tableau captures the plot of a section of the play and, more importantly, the underlying theme and symbolism. Our tableau represents the sub-scene after the play in the play (3.2). It was a wonderful project for me, and I thoroughly enjoyed being the director of this tableau.

Here is an explanation of the tableau:

In order, the portrayed characters are Rosencrantz, crazy Hamlet, sane Hamlet, and Claudius.

We see the increase in height across the characters. This isn’t due to one overall theme; however, each pair does convey meaning. Rosencrantz is shorter than crazy Hamlet because the former cannot follow the wordplay of, and is overall dimmer than the latter. Hamlet is shorter than Claudius because of their difference in power as well as Hamlet’s increasing difficulty in achieving his revenge against Claudius.

We see on crazy Hamlet’s facial expressions a combination of pleasure, contempt, and reproach: pleasure, because he enjoys his wordplay; contempt, because he scorns an useless spy with so little intellect; reproach, because he is upset that his childhood friends are turned upon him by the person he hates and despises.

We move on to the interaction between crazy Hamlet and sane Hamlet. It is clear here that crazy Hamlet is dragging sane Hamlet down, blinding him by covering his eye and making him drop his dagger. Hamlet’s craziness is directed towards side characters, such as R&G and Polonius. He enjoys fooling these characters and demonstrating his mental superiority; however, this activity distracts him from the important action of killing Claudius. Also, it can be argued that Hamlet’s acted craziness is affecting his real sanity.

Finally, we have Claudius. Remember that this is right after the play in the play. Claudius is angry and aghast that he is exposed by Hamlet; he is definitely scared and worried that Hamlet will try to kill him, and he is also feeling remorseful over his wrongdoings. All of this is evident in Claudius’ facial expression and body language.

P1: Solar Cell

2020-04-04

This is a short essay I wrote earlier describing the workings of the basic solar cell.

•–•–•–•–•–•–•

The solar cell converts electromagnetic radiation energy into electrical energy. It utilizes the photovoltaic effect to create electron-hole pairs, which, under the influence of the electric field created by the diode of the cell, moves apart to the opposite ends of the diode. This movement leads to a current when the ends of the diode are connected to an external circuit.

The photovoltaic effect occurs when a photon of sufficient energy is absorbed by a valence electron in an atom, and the electron gains the necessary energy to become unrestrained from the atom and move freely in the material. This effect leads to the creation of a pair of charge carriers: the electron and the positive ion from which the electron left. The ion is viewed as a hole – a position that is missing an electron.

In order to harness the energy of these electron-hole pairs, they need to move in opposite directions to an external circuit. This is where the diode becomes crucial.

Solar cell consists of two distinct semiconducting silicon plates. These plates are doped with small amounts of foreign elements: one contains elements such as phosphorus, antimony, or arsenic so that for each of these foreign atoms, there is an extra electron that is not used to bind with adjacent silicon atoms in the crystal lattice; the other contains elements such as boron, aluminum, and gallium so that for each of these foreign atoms, there is the lack of an electron required to bind with adjacent silicon atoms. These are called the negative and positive type materials, respectively, which is abbreviated to n-type and p-type.

When these two plates are put in contact, a diode is formed. In the contact region of a diode, some of the electrons from the n-type moves to the holes in the p-type. Thus, the n-type becomes positively charged, while the p-type becomes negatively charged. This creates an electric field from the n-type to the p-type, and any free electrons in this region will move to the n-type, while any holes will move to the p-type.

When an electron-hole pair is created by an incident photon through the photovoltaic effect, the electron and the hole will move to the opposite ends of the diode, as described above. Thus, when the ends of the diode are connected to an external circuit, the electron will move through the external circuit before returning to the other end of the diode, instead of simply crossing over to the other side of the diode. The hole moves oppositely to the electron, although at this point, both simply describe the current.

When enough photons of sufficient energy are hitting the solar cell, a significant current would be generated. The direction of the conventional current is from the p-type to the n-type. This is the principle of the solar cell.
